Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions which people ask before surgery and anesthesia procedures

What can i eat and drink before surgery?

You may not eat or drink anything for eight hours before surgery with the exception of some (but not all) medications. Please understand that eating too close to your scheduled surgery may require rescheduling of your surgery.

Is there a possibility that i can wake up during the procedure?

It is highly unlikely. With the use of modern pharmacology and monitoring its not uncommon but waking up during a procedure is extremely rare.

How anesthesiologists manage the post operative pain?

Effective post-operative pain management is crucial for patient comfort, recovery, and overall satisfaction with surgical care. Anesthesiologists use a tailored, patient-centered approach to achieve the best outcomes.

What if a patient develops nausea and get disorentated after waking up?

When a patient develops nausea or becomes disoriented after waking up from anesthesia, anesthesiologists follow a structured approach to manage these symptoms. The anesthesiologist may administer antiemetic drugs to control nausea and vomiting or may work with nursing staff to reassure and reorient the patient, explaining where they are, what has happened, and the expected course of recovery.

If a patinet has a pacemaker or defibrillator?

Make sure that you inform your surgeon and anesthesiologist that you have the device and bring the information card from your device.

Whether the anesthesologist is in-network with the insurance?

If you prefer, you can also provide us with your insurance details, and we can check our records to see if we have an agreement with your insurance company. However, the most accurate and up-to-date information will always come directly from your insurance provider.
